Teams

Volleyball is played between two teams of six players on a court divided by a net. The game begins with a serve, where a player hits the ball over the net to start the rally. The receiving team must then use up to three hits to return the ball, typically using a bump (pass), a set, and a spike. Fun fact, volleyball passes are not required to be made from inside the court boundaries. Players are allowed to run outside the court to play a ball, provided the ball has not yet touched the ground or an object/person outside the court.

Positions

Setter (S)

    The "quarterback" of the team. Responsible for running the offense by delivering the second ball to attackers, usually from the front-right or back-right positions.

Outside Hitter (OH)

    Known as the left-side hitter, they are primary attackers and passers (serve receive). They play on the left side, attacking from position 4 and passing from position 5.

Middle Blocker (MB/MH)

    Typically the tallest player, responsible for blocking opposing hits in the center of the net and attacking "quick" sets. They play in the middle front (position 3) and are often replaced by the libero in the back row.

Opposite Hitter (OPP)

    Plays on the right side of the court, acting as a key attacker and providing crucial blocking against the opponent's outside hitter. They are typically opposite the setter in the rotation.

Libero (L)

    A defensive specialist wearing a different colored jersey, specializing in passing and defense (digging). They only play in the back row, typically for the middle blocker, and do not rotate to the front.

How To Score

Points are scored when a team lands the ball in the opposing court or when the other team makes an error, such as hitting the ball out of bounds or failing to return it over the net. When a point is scored on the opposing team's ball posession, the next point they score, the team will rotate clockwise to the next position on the court. The game is typically played best to five sets to 25 points, win by 2 points.
